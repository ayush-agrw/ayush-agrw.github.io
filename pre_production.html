Overview

Ghost in Leiden is a fast-paced cyberpunk action short film set in a rain-drenched dystopian city lit by neon and secrets. It follows Nadia—a former assassin and gun for hire—who, while breaking into the headquarters of a corrupt tech giant to retrieve classified data, stumbles upon a buried truth about her past. As the city chokes under rain and ruin, Nadia ignites a chain of events that leads to a high-speed aerial chase, not just to destroy the ones who stole everything from her… but to become something else. Not a weapon. Not a ghost. But someone who saves others no matter the cost.

Note: below you will find the results of my brainstorming sessions and the development of my main ideas for the short movie. It is a short documentation of my pre-production work and also includes a screenplay, storyboard snippets, character designs, environment designs + workflow, as well as my image stylization tool that I developed for this project...

Screenplay: Ghost in Leiden

FADE IN:

Leiden Cityscape – Night

The camera sweeps over the neon-lit streets of Leiden as a train zooms past in the distance. Skyscrapers pierce the dark, clouded sky. The sound of rain and distant city noises fill the air. The scene is a mix of electric blues and toxic greens, casting an eerie glow on the wet streets below.

Neon Billboard – Night

Nadia, 20, sleek in a matte-black and pink bodysuit with a straight fringe, sits atop a neon billboard. Holographic screens float around her as her fingers dance through the air. The city hums beneath her.

Jax, wearing a threaded cloak and with silver circuit-like lines on his skin, tosses a small device between his hands. He watches Nadia intently.

Jax (softly)

"Are you sure about this, Nadia?"

Nadia smirks, not breaking focus.

Nadia (confident)

"I'll be in and out before they even notice."

Jax nods, his concern evident.

Jax
"Just... don't do anything too crazy. I wouldn't want a friend dying on me."

Nadia stands, the holograms vanishing. She powers up her suit, the red slits glowing brightly.

Nadia (teasing)

"Crazy? Me? Never."

She leaps off the billboard, her silhouette framed by the neon lights, and disappears into the night.

City Streets – Night

Nadia plummets through the rain, activating her flight suit. Thrusters flare as she glides through the narrow alleys and between towering skyscrapers. The city below is a blur of colors and movement.

Flashback

A sequence of dimly lit memories —

Nadia, younger, clad in a crude prototype of her bodysuit, steps over bodies in a blood-streaked alley. Her eyes are cold. A target pleads for his life. She silences him with brutal precision.

Another flash — Nadia walks away from a burning building. Her hands tremble. She’s alone. Always alone.

Then —

A quiet café, sunlight streaming in. Nadia, tense and out of place, meets eyes with a man — kind, warm, unaware of her past.

A montage follows:

Laughter over drinks. Quiet mornings. Long walks through narrow city streets.

The final flash —

A news feed showing a protest crushed by corporate soldiers. Nadia, watching, jaw clenched. The man beside her puts a hand on hers. She closes her eyes.

Nadia (V.O., determined)

"So I chose. No more contracts. No more killing. If I’m going to fight… it’s going to mean something now."

CyberHelix HQ – Night

Nadia hovers outside the towering, ominous building. The HQ absorbs the light around it, making it appear as a dark fortress.

Nadia (V.O., calculating)

"Too risky to go from the top."

A soft beep. Nadia glances at her HUD.

Jax (V.O., urgent)

"I've jammed the ground signals, but you don’t have long."

Nadia dives toward the lower levels, her movements precise. She lands silently behind a delivery truck entering a side entrance.

CyberHelix HQ – Interior – Night

Nadia moves swiftly through the dimly lit corridors, her bodysuit blending into the shadows. She bypasses security checkpoints with ease, her eyes scanning for threats.

She attaches a small device to a scanner. The lights flicker off. She slips through.

R&D Department – Night

Nadia enters a dark room lined with rows of terminals and screens. The atmosphere is cold, sterile. The central server hums ominously.

She sits down at a terminal. Her fingers move quickly across the holographic keyboard. Streams of encrypted data flood the screen.

The screen flashes as the encryption unlocks. Nadia's eyes widen as she sees a directory labeled "Project Helix 4".

Her hand hovers over the orb she was sent to retrieve but pauses when she spots another orb — labeled with her brother's name.

Flashback

The memory of her brother being taken by CyberHelix operatives. The horror on his face as they drag him away.

Nadia (V.O., whispering, emotional)

"They took him... they killed him."

Nadia's eyes harden. She grabs both the target data and her brother’s memory.

She activates a virus on the mainframe. The screen turns red, counting down.

Alarm blares. Guards burst into the room.

Nadia’s face sets in determination. She draws her energy blades.

Action Sequence

Nadia fights with fluid, lethal precision, taking down guards one by one. Her movements are a blur of speed and skill. The countdown continues as she fights, her breath ragged, her muscles straining.

A blow hits Nadia’s head. She stumbles, her vision blurring. A vision of her brother flashes — his smile, his hand reaching toward her.

Nadia rises, fierce and unstoppable. She sends the virus through the network, triggering the self-destruct.

CyberHelix HQ – Exterior – Night

Nadia leaps through a window as the building explodes behind her. She soars through the city, chased by CyberHelix guards in flight suits. The chase is intense. The city is a dizzying blur beneath them.

Nadia dodges between buildings, her suit overheating, thrusters failing. She leads the guards into a wind turbine field, cutting her engines and free-falling.

The guards overshoot, some caught in the turbines, exploding.

Nadia reactivates her thrusters. They sputter.

Crash.

Nadia lands hard in a dark alley, rolling to a stop. She’s exhausted, barely able to move.

A guard lands nearby, battered but alive, weapon raised.

Nadia smiles faintly, knowing she has completed her mission.

Whiteout

Nadia is bathed in warm, ethereal light. She looks up, seeing her brother, his hand outstretched.

Tears well in Nadia’s eyes as she takes his hand.

Together, they walk into the light — leaving the pain and darkness behind.

FADE OUT.

Environment Design Workflow

This layered environment workflow allows for fast iteration and a handcrafted aesthetic while maintaining depth, motion, and stylized lighting.

Blockout and Modeling

Begin by speed-modeling the environment from the camera’s perspective using basic geometric shapes in Blender. Use procedural city generation via Geometry Nodes for large-scale urban layouts. Add detailed assets from Quixel Megascans to enrich key areas and foregrounds.

Basic Materials, Lighting and Texture Painting

Apply basic materials and lighting for structure and readability. Perform stylized texture painting directly in Blender with graphic brushwork for key surfaces (walls, streets, props), lighting accents, weathering, and hand-drawn surface effects. Simulate lighting with hand-painted light maps where 3D lighting isn’t expressive enough. Include emissive particles (glow, dust, smog) for atmosphere.

Graphic Reduction (Skipped in Example)

Use Grease Pencil for simplified blobs and shapes in background and foreground. Design opaque background silhouettes and translucent foreground elements to guide the eye and increase depth.

Stylization Tool

Layer in stylized effects (e.g., halftone textures, chromatic aberration, noise effects, vignettes, automatic graphic reduction for background elements, paint smears, line hatching) for a sophisticated hybrid 2D/3D feel. This also gives the movie a heavily stylized / post-processed look, similar to the Spiderverse movies. Compare this result to the picture above...

Color Grading

Render multiple passes (base render, depth, texture overlays, particles). Using DaVinci Resolve or Blender, tweak the image contrast, sharpness, and lift + gamma + gain. Emphasize light/dark areas with digital painting.

Stylization Tool

This was definitely the most interesting and uncertain part of the pre-production process. The goal was to give my animated scenes a distinct look and feel that stays coherent throughout the movie. The inspiration for this was mostly the heavily stylized and post-processed look of Spider-Man Into the Spiderverse, as well as certain cyberpunk scenes.
